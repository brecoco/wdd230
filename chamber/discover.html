Discover the city of São Paulo

About São Paulo

São Paulo is a Brazilian municipality, capital of the homonymous state and main financial, corporate and commercial center in South America. It is the most populous city in Brazil, in the American continent, in the Portuguese-speaking world and in the entire southern hemisphere. São Paulo is the most influential Brazilian city on the global stage, being, in 2016, the 11th most globalized city on the planet. Founded in 1554 by Jesuit priests, the city is world-renowned and exerts strong national influence and significant international importance, whether from a cultural, economic or political point of view.

São Paulo's Population

"The city is the 8th most populous on the planet and its metropolitan region, with around 21 million inhabitants, is the 10th largest urban agglomeration in the world. The capital of São Paulo also has a cosmopolitan character, given that, in 2016, it had native residents from 196 different countries. Regions around Greater São Paulo are also metropolises, such as Campinas, Baixada Santista and Vale do Paraíba. In 2021, it was considered by the Boston Consulting Group as one of the 45 best cities in the world to live in."

Location of São Paulo

São Paulo is the capital of the most populous state in Brazil, São Paulo, located at latitude 23°33'01'' south and longitude 46°38'02'' west. The total area of the municipality is 1,521.11 km², according to the Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics (IBGE), being the ninth largest in the state in terms of territorial extension. Of the entire area of the municipality, 949,611 km² are urban areas (2015), being the largest urban area in the country.

São Paulo's Finances

The metropolis has the 23rd largest GDP in the world, representing, alone, 11% of the entire Brazilian GDP, 34% of the state's GDP, as well as 36% of all its production of goods and services, in addition to being the seat of 63% of multinationals established in Brazil, being responsible for 28% of all national scientific production in 2005, and for more than 40% of patents produced in the country. The city is also home to B3 (Brazil, Bolsa, Balcão), the 5th largest stock exchange in the world by market capitalization (2017 data).
