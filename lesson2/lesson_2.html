Lesson 02: Responsive Design Principles Document

White Space and Clean Design

Amazon

Sales pages, especially those with a wide variety of products, need to make good use of the space they have to present many things to customers in a clear and direct way. For this, it is necessary that the information is well distributed on the screen, making use of the blank space to not leave the page polluted in such a way that the customer does not understand what is being shown or that he misses something that he could have taken advantage of but not did it because he hadn't seen it.

PARC: Repetition

Nike

Product sales page, mainly similar products, I believe they are the ones that most need repetition. Products need to be arranged in such a way that everything is easy to see: name, color, sizes, style, price. These characteristics are repeated in all products on the page. I took the Nike website as an example, where in the selection of sneakers we can clearly see the photo of the sneakers, availability, model name, genre, number of colors and price, and all this repeating for each item.

PARC: Alignment

LDS Library

The church library page for me is a good example of item alignment and organization. The alignment of the items is very well done, leaving the appearance pleasant and easy to navigate. Items are properly sized for easy selection.
